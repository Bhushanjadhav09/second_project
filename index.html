About India

India Information

India is a land of various cultures and a rich heritage. It is the seventh-largest country by area and the second-most populous country globally. The peacock is India's national bird, and the Bengal tiger is the country's national animal. The national song is named Vande Matram (written by Bankimchandra Chatterji).

India has a unique culture and is one of the oldest and greatest civilizations of the world. India has achieved all-round socio-economic progress since its Independence. India covers an area of 32,87,263 sq. km, extending from the snow-covered Himalayan heights to the tropical rain forests of the south.

The naming of the country was debated by the drafting committee of the constitution. Before the Constitutional Assembly in 1949, the country was known as Bharat, India, and Hindustan. While a good number of the drafting committee members preferred the old name, Bharat, many others favoured India. .India is now the fastest growing major economy in the world. Diversity

India is known for its remarkable diversity, including a wide range of cultural, linguistic, religious, ethnic, and geographic variations. Here are some aspects of diversity in India: Language: With 22 officially recognized languages, including Hindi, Bengali, Telugu, Marathi, Tamil, Urdu, Gujarati, Malayalam, Kannada, Oriya, Punjabi, Assamese, Maithili, and others, India is a linguistically varied nation. Religious: Major religions including Hinduism, Islam, Christianity, Sikhism, Buddhism, and Jainism coexist in India, a country renowned for its religious tolerance. The diversity of religions is further enhanced by different sects and customs within each major faith. Historical and Architectural Diversity: India's history is characterized by a multitude of dynasties, empires, and rulers, who have produced a wide range of historical landmarks and architectural styles. India has a rich history that includes the Mughal architecture of the Taj Mahal and the ancient temples of Khajuraho. Economy Diversity: Various states have various roles for the agricultural, industry, and services sectors when it comes to economic activity.
